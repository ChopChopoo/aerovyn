Frequently asked questions

Common questions about Aerovyn shoes and ordering.

Can I use the Pace as a daily trainer?

The Pace is built for workouts, not easy miles. Most runners find it too firm for daily use. We recommend a softer trainer for easy days and the Pace for two or three workouts per week.

How does the Pace compare to a carbon-plated racer?

The Pace has no carbon plate and a lower stack. It is built for repeat efforts where a race shoe would be overkill and would wear out quickly.

How many miles should I expect from the Pace?

We design the Pace for 300 to 400 miles of workout use. Used on easy days as well, expect closer to 250 miles.

What is the difference between the Pace and the Pace XT?

The XT has 2 mm more stack and slightly more cushioning, for workouts longer than about 40 minutes.

Is the Sprint a track shoe?

No — the Sprint is a road racer. For track racing we recommend a true track spike, which we do not currently make.

Where are Aerovyn shoes made?

Materials are sourced in Vietnam and Japan. Final assembly is done in Vietnam. Each pair is QA-tested in Flagstaff.
